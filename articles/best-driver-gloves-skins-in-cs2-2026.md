## FAQ

### What are Driver Gloves in CS2?

Driver Gloves are one of eight glove models added to CS2 (and CS:GO before it) as part of the Glove Collection, originally introduced with Operation Hydra in 2017. Like all gloves, they're purely cosmetic — equipping any glove model has zero effect on recoil, movement speed, or any other gameplay stat, so the choice comes down entirely to looks and budget.

### What is the cheapest Driver Gloves skin?

As of June 2026, Racing Green is the cheapest Driver Gloves finish, with a Field-Tested copy going for around $35 on [Skin.Land](https://skin.land). ### What is the most expensive Driver Gloves skin?

King Snake and Garden are currently the priciest Driver Gloves finishes, both reaching into the $2,000–$2,700 range for a Factory New copy. For context on how Driver Gloves compare to other models at the top end, check our roundup of the [most expensive gloves in CS2](https://skin.land/blog/the-most-expensive-gloves-in-csgo/).

### Does the finish on Driver Gloves affect gameplay?

No. All glove skins, including every Driver Gloves finish, are cosmetic only. They don't change recoil patterns, movement speed, sound cues, or anything else that affects how you play — the only thing that changes is how your hands look in first-person and on the kill feed.

### Which wear should I buy for Driver Gloves?

It depends on the finish. Pattern-heavy finishes like King Snake, Imperial Plaid, and Brocade Crane keep their look fairly consistent across wears, so Battle-Scarred or Well-Worn often gives you 90% of the visual for a fraction of the price. Solid-color or high-contrast finishes show wear more obviously, so Field-Tested or better is worth the extra cost there. ### Are Driver Gloves a good investment?

Driver Gloves can hold value well, especially rarer finishes like King Snake and Garden, but gloves in general are more volatile than knives since they're not tied to a single rarity tier across all models.
